WalletNest reads Ethereum wallets and gives you back clean, predictable JSON. No smart contracts, no writes, no signing. You hand it an address; it hands you what that address holds.

Under the hood it talks to [Alchemy](https://alchemy.com) and normalizes the response, so you get the same shape whether a token has metadata or not, and whether a wallet has one NFT or a thousand.

**It reads the chain with your Alchemy key, not ours.** Every request carries your own key, so your usage is billed to your account and metered on your own plan. See [Authentication](/authentication).

## What you can do

| Endpoint | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| `GET /v1/wallets/{address}` | Everything about one wallet |
| `POST /v1/portfolio` | Up to 25 wallets, aggregated into portfolio totals |

## What it does not do

Being clear about the edges up front saves you an integration bug later.

- **You supply the Alchemy key.** There is no free data tier. Get one at [dashboard.alchemy.com](https://dashboard.alchemy.com).
- **Ethereum mainnet only.** No L2s, no testnets.
- **No pagination in v1.** NFTs cap at 20 per wallet, transfers at 15. The `totalNftCount` in a portfolio response counts what was *returned*, not what is *owned*.
- **No prices.** Balances are token quantities, not fiat values.
- **Numbers are for display.** Balances arrive as JSON numbers, already scaled by `decimals`. A balance large enough to exceed IEEE-754's safe integer range will lose precision. Don't settle trades on them.

## Base URL

```
https://wallet-nest-nine.vercel.app/api/v1
```

The machine-readable spec is served, unauthenticated, from [`/api/v1/openapi.json`](https://wallet-nest-nine.vercel.app/api/v1/openapi.json). Point your client generator straight at it.
